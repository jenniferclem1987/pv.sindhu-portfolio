P V SINDHU

Pusarla Venkata Sindhu

Indian Badminton Player

About

Pusarla Venkata Sindhu is an Indian badminton player. Considered one of India's most successful sportspersons, Sindhu has won medals at various tournaments such as the Olympics and on the BWF circuit, including a gold at the 2019 World Championships.

Born: 5 July 1995 (age 28 years), Hyderabad

Parents: P.V. Ramana, P. Vijaya

Coach: Kim Ji-hyun (2019)

Teammates:Lakshya Sen, Srikanth Kidambi, Chirag Shetty

Awards:Padma Bhushan, Padma Shri

Nominations:CNN-IBN Indian of the Year in Sports

The shuttler remains India's most successful female Olympian.

PV Sindhu has already completed more than a decade on the BWF international circuit, having breached the top 20 in the women's singles BWF rankings at the age of 17. India's most successful female Olympian and only one of the three (Norman Pritchard and Sushil Kumar being the other two) to win two medals in individual events for the country, PV Sindhu has established herself as a force to reckon with. While she may not be at her best right now, she has all the potential one needs to deliver at the biggest stages. Sindhu never lets the occasion get to her. No matter who the opponent is and where she is playing, if the stakes are high, count on the Hyderabadi to punch above her weight and take everyone by surprise. She is a monster mentally, one of the very few to have matched the Chinese toe-to-toe.

Her Inspiration -Coach Pullela Gopichand. While her mom is her main inspiration, as she taught her to fight for her dreams and give each task her best, Pullela Gopichand's victory during the 2001 All England Open Badminton Championships, inspired Sindhu to start playing badminton.
